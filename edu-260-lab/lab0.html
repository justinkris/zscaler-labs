OVERVIEW

Welcome to the Introduction to Troubleshooting — Part 2 of 2 (EDU-260) Hands-on Lab. You play the role of a Level 1 HelpDesk analyst at Safemarch, investigating reported issues using read-only access to ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX admin portals. By the end of this lab series you will be able to:

  • Verify client connectivity and trace ZIA and ZPA SAML authentication flows
  • Capture and interpret ZCC packet logs to diagnose traffic-forwarding problems
  • Review SSL inspection certificates and identify misconfigured ZIA access policies
  • Confirm threat protection, sandbox, Browser Isolation, Firewall, and DNS control behaviour
  • Investigate DLP rule mismatches using block logs and dictionary inspection
  • Diagnose user-experience issues by correlating ZDX scores, Cloud Path data, and User Device Events
  • Verify NSS server and feed configuration for SIEM connectivity

💭 THINK FIRST · LAB ENVIRONMENT

This course intentionally restricts your portal access to Read-Only. Understanding why that constraint exists — and how to work effectively within it — is itself a troubleshooting skill.

  • You access ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX admin portals as a Read-Only Administrator. What specific actions does that role prevent, and why is that restriction realistic for a Tier 1 HelpDesk analyst?
    Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
    Read-Only prevents saving configuration changes — you can view all policies, logs, and settings but cannot modify them. This is realistic because Tier 1 analysts diagnose and escalate; they don't change production policy. It also prevents accidental mis-configuration during a training lab where multiple students share tenant infrastructure.
  • This lab uses seven distinct department user accounts (IT, HR, MKT, CON, OT, SALES, plus ADMIN). Why do multiple personas matter in a troubleshooting course rather than just using one admin account?
    Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
    Policy in ZIA and ZPA is often scoped to specific users or departments. A block that affects the IT department user might not affect HR, which means the root cause is a user-group policy assignment, not an infrastructure issue. Multiple personas let you reproduce user-specific issues, compare policy application across groups, and test whether a problem is universal or identity-specific.

Before You Start

  • Assumed prior knowledge: Completion of EDU-259 (Introduction to Troubleshooting — Part 1 of 2) or equivalent. You should be familiar with the Zscaler troubleshooting methodology — Gather, Identify, Plan, Implement, Verify — and comfortable navigating ZIA Web Insights and ZPA Diagnostics logs.
  • Environment needed: A Skytap lab pod containing at least one Windows Client PC VM, plus read-only admin credentials for a shared Zscaler tenant (thezerotrustexchange.com domain). All seven department user accounts and the Session Password are provided in the Resources tab of your Skytap environment.
  • Estimated total lab time: Approximately 4.5 hours across all 8 labs. Lab 2 (Authentication) and Lab 5 (Security Services) are the longest at ~40–45 min each. Lab 1 (environment setup) and Lab 8 (NSS) are the shortest at ~15 min each.

Lab Description and Scenario

You are a member of the Level 1 HelpDesk team at Safemarch. Users from multiple departments report Zscaler-related issues — authentication failures, traffic not forwarding, unexpected policy blocks, poor performance, and logging gaps. Your job is to diagnose each issue using the read-only admin portals, ZCC diagnostics, and browser developer tools, then produce a finding that could be escalated to a Tier 2 engineer.

Labs build on each other sequentially. You will use a single Corp: Client PC VM as your test endpoint throughout, switching between department user identities as needed by each lab scenario.

Lab Topology

Your Skytap pod provides one Corp: Client PC VM running Windows. ZCC is pre-installed on the Client PC. The ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX admin portals are cloud services accessible from any browser — some tasks require you to open them from inside the Client PC VM to simulate a corporate endpoint, while others can be done from your local laptop browser.
