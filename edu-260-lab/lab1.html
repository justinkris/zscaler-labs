💭 THINK FIRST · LAB ENVIRONMENT

Before touching a live environment, a good troubleshooter builds a mental map of what should be there — topology, roles, access constraints.

  • What three types of resources does your Skytap training pod give you access to, and why does that separation matter for troubleshooting?
  • You can access ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX Admin Portals as a Read-Only Administrator. What does this restriction prevent you from doing, and how does it affect the labs?
  • What would happen to your ZCC enrollment and ongoing tests if the Corp: Client PC VM shuts down mid-lab?
Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
Your pod gives you: (1) a Corp: Client PC virtual machine for end-user simulation, (2) admin/user credentials for all Zscaler services, and (3) access to ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX Admin Portals. The separation matters because real-world issues span all three layers — you often need to correlate what the client is seeing with what the admin portal logs are showing.
Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
Read-Only access means you can view all configuration, policies, and logs — but cannot save changes. In these labs that is intentional: you verify that existing configurations are correct, rather than making changes. It mirrors a real HelpDesk Tier 1 scenario where the analyst needs to diagnose before escalating to someone with write access.
Q3 — MODEL ANSWER
ZCC enrollment state is stored on the VM. If the VM shuts down, ZCC loses its session and must re-authenticate when restarted. Ongoing packet captures would be lost. For labs that span multiple tasks this means you may need to re-enroll the IT_department_user after a restart — always check Service Status in ZCC before continuing.

LAB 1

Scenario: You are a member of the Level 1 HelpDesk team at Safemarch. Before troubleshooting any Zscaler issue you need to confirm your virtual environment is running and locate your credentials.

Time: ~15 minutes
Environment: Skytap + Zscaler Portals (Read-Only Admin)

Task 1.1: Test Your Lab Access and Start Your Environment

1 Access the Lab Environment

  1. On your laptop, open a browser window and navigate to the Skytap Access URL provided in your lab confirmation email.
  2. Enter your Skytap Passcode and click Go.
  3. If the Corp: Client PC VM is shown as shut down, click to start the VM.
  4. When prompted for Windows credentials, log in:
    • Username: Student
    • Password: Admin-123!
  5. If your machine is locked, click the key icon 🔑 to display usernames and passwords saved with the VM. Click Insert to paste into the VM.
  6. If the virtual desktop is too small or too large, click the resize icon to fit the frame to your current browser window.

Note: The ZIA, ZPA, and ZDX Admin Portals are cloud services accessible from any machine with an Internet connection. In some labs you should access these URLs from inside the Client PC VM to simulate a corporate device.
