💡 THINK FIRST · TEAMS CALL QUALITY

Call quality metrics (MOS Score, jitter, packet loss) are fundamentally different from web probe metrics. A low MOS score tells you how the call sounds to participants; Cloud Path hop latency tells you why.

  • What is a MOS (Mean Opinion Score) and why does a score of 2.06 indicate a problem? What MOS value is considered acceptable for voice communications?
  • Audio Latency is 640ms. The Hop View shows 569ms of that is between the device and the local gateway. What local network conditions could cause 569ms of latency on a single wired or wireless hop?
  • Grady Archie has two devices listed in the session. Why would you check all devices rather than just looking at the first one listed?
Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
MOS (Mean Opinion Score) is a 1–5 scale measuring perceived audio quality in voice communications, where 5 is perfect and 1 is unintelligible. A score below 3.5 is noticeable to users; below 3.0 is poor. 2.06 means participants are likely hearing choppy, distorted audio. Acceptable voice-over-IP quality starts at MOS ≥ 4.0. The threshold for “toll-quality” (indistinguishable from a phone call) is MOS ≥ 4.3.
Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
Possible causes for 569ms latency to the local gateway: (1) Wi-Fi congestion or weak signal — the device may be on a 2.4GHz band with interference. (2) A faulty or overloaded Wi-Fi access point causing queuing delay. (3) A network configuration issue such as a switch port running at half-duplex, causing constant packet collisions. (4) Physical cable damage if wired.
Q3 — MODEL ANSWER
Different devices can have completely different network configurations, drivers, and connection states. In this example, Grady Archie’s first device does not include Microsoft Teams Call Quality in its application list — meaning it was not used for the call. Only the second device (GRADY-PC) has the Teams Call Quality data. If you had only checked the first device, you would have found nothing.

Task 6.3: Locate Meeting Issues

LAB 6 PROGRESS
QUICK REFERENCE ▲
MOS Score
Mean Opinion Score: 1–5 scale for voice quality. MOS ≥ 4.0 = good, 3.5–4.0 = acceptable, <3.5 = noticeable degradation.
Audio Latency
End-to-end delay for audio packets. Values above 150ms become perceptible; above 400ms severely impacts conversation quality.
Meeting Monitoring Metrics
Per-participant metrics captured during a Teams call: Audio MOS in/out, Audio Latency in/out, Audio Jitter, Audio After-Input.
Teams Transport Relay
Microsoft cloud relay server used for Teams media. The Cloud Path probe measures the hop-by-hop path from the endpoint to this relay.
