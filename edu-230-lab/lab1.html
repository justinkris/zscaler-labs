💭 THINK FIRST · LAB 1

Understanding your baseline security posture before applying any controls is the first step in any security programme — you cannot measure improvement without a starting point.

  • What does a failed result on securitypreview.zscaler.com tell you about unprotected internet access?
  • Why is establishing a baseline posture score important before making any configuration changes?
  • What types of threats does a Ransomware Risk Assessment typically flag on an endpoint with no web security?
Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
Failed tests reveal specific threat categories the unprotected device is vulnerable to — malware downloads, phishing, botnet callbacks, and XSS. Each failure is a real attack vector that Zscaler policies will close throughout this course.
Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
A baseline lets you measure the real impact of each policy change. Without it, you cannot prove to stakeholders that the investment reduced risk — you need a before/after comparison to demonstrate value.
Q3 — MODEL ANSWER
Typically: unrestricted file downloads including executables, access to known malicious domains, no blocking of C2 callback URLs, and no inspection of encrypted traffic where ransomware payloads often hide.

Task 1.1: Test Cyber Risk Posture on Unprotected Device

  1. Open a browser on the Corp: Client PC and navigate to securitypreview.zscaler.com.
  2. Click Test your cyber risk posture and review the results — note which tests fail. These will be remediated throughout the labs.
  3. In a new tab, navigate to https://www.testmydefenses.com/.
  4. Select Ransomware Risk Assessment and review the Critical results.
  5. Record your findings — you will revisit these sites at the end of Lab 4 to confirm improvement.
