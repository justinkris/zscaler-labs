LAB 1

Scenario: Before configuring Zscaler policies, you need to access the tenant environment and test your unprotected cyber risk posture as a baseline for the improvements made throughout the course.

⏱ 15 min 📋 1 task ● SETUP
🤔 Think First — Before You Click 3 questions to prime your thinking ▾

The failed tests reveal specific threat categories the unprotected device is vulnerable to — malware downloads, phishing, botnet callbacks, and XSS. Each failure is a real attack vector that Zscaler policies will close throughout this course.

A baseline lets you measure the real impact of each policy change. Without it, you cannot prove to stakeholders that the security investment reduced risk — you need a before/after comparison.

Typically: unrestricted file downloads including executables, access to known malicious domains, no blocking of Command-and-Control (C2) callback URLs, and no inspection of encrypted traffic where ransomware payloads often hide.

TASK 1.1

Task 1.1: Test Cyber Risk Posture on Unprotected Device

In this task you will test your cyber risk posture before any Zscaler protection is applied, establishing a baseline to compare against at the end of the course.

  1. Open a browser on the Corp: Client PC and go to securitypreview.zscaler.com.
  2. Click Test your cyber risk posture.
  3. Review the results — note which tests fail (these will be remediated throughout the labs).
  4. In a new tab, go to https://www.testmydefenses.com/.
  5. Select Ransomware Risk Assessment and review the Critical results.
  6. Take note of these findings — you will revisit these sites in Lab 4 to compare your improved security posture.
