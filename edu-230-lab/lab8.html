Lab 8: Deception-Based Active Defense

LAB 8

Scenario: Zscaler Deception deploys fake decoy assets throughout the environment. Any attacker interaction with these hidden decoys is a high-confidence breach indicator. In this lab you simulate attacker reconnaissance against public decoys, then investigate the resulting alerts in the Deception Admin Console.

⏱ 30 min 📋 2 tasks ● ZIA
🤔 Think First — Before You Click 3 questions to prime your thinking ▾

Legitimate users and applications have no reason to ever access decoy assets — they are invisible to authorized traffic. Any interaction with a decoy definitively means unauthorized exploration (reconnaissance, lateral movement, or credential harvesting). IDS alerts, by contrast, fire on legitimate traffic patterns that resemble attacks, generating false positives.

Deception captures: attacker IP address, decoy name and type, recon method (web login attempt, network scan, etc.), request URI, entered credentials (username/password attempted), timestamp, and optionally the source user identity if ZCC is running. This provides attribution data without requiring the attacker to succeed.

ZIA and ZPA focus on north-south traffic (user-to-internet and user-to-private-apps). Deception addresses east-west lateral movement after a breach — when an attacker is already inside the network. Together: ZIA/ZPA prevent initial compromise; Deception detects post-breach movement before the attacker reaches real assets.

TASK 8.1

Task 8.1: Generate Recon Activity

Simulate the activities of an attacker doing reconnaissance against public-facing decoy assets.

SDC Lab — Read-Only: Switch to the Solutions Demo Center (SDC) tenant. Credentials are provided in the Resources panel of your lab environment.
  1. On the Corp: Client PC, log out of Zscaler Client Connector.
  2. Log back in with the SDC Marketing department user:
    1. Enter the MKT_department_username and click Login.
    2. At the IdP prompt, enter the username again (if not pre-filled) and click Next.
    3. Enter the Session_Password and click Sign in.
  3. Open a browser and navigate to one of the public-facing decoys:
    • http://wordpress.thezerotrustexchange.com/
    • http://helpdesk.thezerotrustexchange.com/
  4. At the login page, enter fake credentials to record recon activity in the logs:
    • Username: sdcuser_<identifier>__ (include a unique string so you can find your log entry)
    • Password: SDCaccess
  5. Click Sign On. The page will appear to clear and re-prompt — this is the decoy recording your activity.
    Note: This is expected behavior. The decoy has logged your credentials and source IP. In a real attack scenario, this would trigger an alert to the SOC.

TASK 8.2

Task 8.2: Investigate Deception Alerts

Sign in to the Deception Administrator Console on the SDC tenant and investigate the recon event you just generated.

SDC Lab — Read-Only: Use your Admin credentials for the SDC tenant.
  1. Go to Solutions Demo Center (https://sdc.zslogin.net) and click the Solutions Demo Center Login tile.
  2. Enter your admin credentials:
    • Login ID: Admin_User_ID
    • Password: Session_Password
  3. Click the Deception tile and accept the End User Subscription Agreement.
  4. Click Investigate in the main menu and select ThreatParse.
  5. Set the time filter to Last 10 minutes to see the most recent logs.
  6. Search for your recon event using the query:
    decoy.name is "moveit.thezerotrustexchange.com"
    (substitute the name of the decoy that was visited)
  7. Also try other query types to explore the data:
    • web.method
    • web.host
    • web.request
  8. Press Enter to run the query and click on any of the attackers displayed to see the Event summary.
  9. Click View Extended Details to see more information about the event.
  10. Click the Event Logs tab and review the detailed log entries.
  11. Sort or search by recon post_data user to find the specific log entry based on the username you entered at the decoy.
    Tip: If the recon post_data user column is not visible, click the chevron icon at the top left of the table to add it.
