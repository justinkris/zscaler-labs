Lab 0: About the EDU-230 Hands-On Lab

OVERVIEW

Welcome to the Cyberthreat Protection (EDU-230) Hands-on Lab. You will practice layered threat defense skills from the EDU-230 e-learning by configuring a real Zscaler Zero Trust Exchange tenant. By the end of this lab series you will be able to:

  • Configure SSL/TLS inspection to gain visibility into encrypted threats
  • Deploy DNS security controls to block malicious domain resolution
  • Apply URL filtering and Cloud App Control to restrict risky web categories and applications
  • Enable Browser Isolation to protect users browsing risky or uncategorised sites
  • Route ZPA private-access traffic through ZIA for full content inspection
  • Use Sandbox file analysis and real-time alerting to detect advanced threats
Course: EDU-230
Platform: Skytap + Zscaler Tenant (safemarch.com)
Labs: 8 total · ~4 hours estimated
💭 THINK FIRST · LAB SETUP

Understanding the environment before you configure anything prevents mis-diagnosis. The EDU-230 lab has two VMs and a pre-provisioned tenant — knowing which machine does what stops you chasing phantom problems in later labs.

  • Lab 1 asks you to visit securitypreview.zscaler.com before enabling any Zscaler policies. Why does this baseline step matter, and what would you lose if you skipped it and configured SSL inspection first?
    Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
    The baseline reveals which specific threats the unprotected device is vulnerable to — malware downloads, C2 callbacks, phishing, XSS. Without it you cannot prove to stakeholders that later policy changes improved the posture. Configuring SSL inspection first would change the test results and you would lose the before/after comparison that validates every subsequent lab.
  • This course uses a single Corp: Client PC VM — there is no App Connector VM pre-deployed as in EDU-200. What does Lab 6 (ZPA Traffic Inspection via ZIA) imply about how the App Connector is provisioned for this course?
    Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
    Lab 6 uses an SDC (Solutions Demo Center) read-only tenant with a pre-configured App Connector, rather than requiring the student to install and register one. This means Labs 2–5 focus purely on ZIA configuration using the Client PC, and the ZPA/SIPA scenario in Lab 6 relies on pre-existing connector infrastructure in the SDC environment — giving you the realistic log data without the provisioning overhead.

Before You Start

  • Assumed prior knowledge: Completion of EDU-200 (Zscaler for Users — Administrator) or equivalent familiarity with ZIA, ZPA, and Zscaler Client Connector basics. You should be able to log into the Experience Center and navigate to Policy sections without guidance.
  • Environment needed: A Skytap lab pod (provisioned by your instructor or self-service lab portal) containing at least one Windows Client PC VM, plus a Zscaler tenant pre-provisioned on the safemarch.com domain. Labs 7 and 8 additionally require access to an SDC (Solutions Demo Center) read-only tenant — credentials are provided in the Resources tab of your lab environment.
  • Estimated total lab time: Approximately 4 hours across all 8 labs. Individual lab times are shown on each lab card in the hub. Labs 2 and 6 are the longest (~45–50 min each); Lab 1 is the shortest (~15 min).

Lab Description and Scenario

Your organization (Safemarch) has deployed the Zscaler Zero Trust Exchange and you are tasked with building out a layered cyberthreat protection strategy. Starting from a completely unprotected baseline, you will progressively add SSL inspection, DNS security, URL filtering, browser isolation, ZPA-to-ZIA inspection, sandbox analysis, and real-time alerting until the endpoint passes all security posture checks.

Labs 7 and 8 use a read-only SDC tenant with pre-populated data so you can explore sandbox file analysis and Zscaler Deception alerting even without triggering live malware events.

Lab Environment

You will have access to a Skytap Pod containing a single Corp: Client PC VM, and a dedicated Zscaler tenant on the safemarch.com domain. You access ZIA, ZPA, and ZIdentity through the Experience Center (console.zscaler.com). Your Student ID is the prefix of your FQDN (e.g., zs9901 from zs9901.safemarch.com).

Virtual Machines and Portals

  • Corp: Client PC — Windows 10 endpoint. This is the machine where you install ZCC, browse the web for threat tests, and confirm policy enforcement. Login: Student / Admin-123!
  • Corp: Win Svr 2016 — Active Directory and DNS for the PATRAINING domain. Used in Lab 6 for ZPA private application access. Login: PATRAINING\Administrator / Admin-123!
  • ZIA Admin Portal — Accessible via Experience Center or directly. URL varies by tenant cloud (e.g., admin.zscalerthree.net). Admin login: student@<Student FQDN>
  • ZPA Admin Portal — Accessible via Experience Center. Used in Lab 6 to configure App Connector groups and access policies.
  • ZIdentity Portal — <StudentID>safemarch.zslogin.net. Used in Lab 1 for initial sign-in and in later labs for user/group management.
  • SDC Tenant — sdc.zslogin.net. Read-only access. Credentials provided via email or the Resources tab in your Skytap environment. Used in Labs 7 and 8.

Student FQDN
Your unique tenant domain, e.g. zs9901.safemarch.com. Used as the domain in all admin login IDs.
ZIdentity URL
<StudentID>safemarch.zslogin.net — your IdP portal for initial sign-in and user provisioning.
SSL Inspection
ZIA decrypts TLS, inspects content, then re-encrypts with Zscaler CA. Requires the Zscaler root cert trusted on the endpoint.
DNS Security
ZIA resolves all DNS on behalf of the user. Malicious or blocked domains return a deny response instead of an IP address.
Browser Isolation
Renders web content in a remote cloud browser. The endpoint only receives a pixel stream — no active content executes locally.
Cloud App Control
Application-layer policy that identifies SaaS apps by DPI fingerprint regardless of port or FQDN.
SIPA
Secure Internet and Private Access — routes ZPA App Connector internet traffic through ZIA for consistent policy enforcement.
SDC Tenant
Solutions Demo Center — shared read-only Zscaler tenant with pre-populated data. Used in Labs 7 and 8.
