LAB 12

Scenario: Use the Risk360 portal in the SDC tenant to explore how Zscaler maps your security posture against MITRE ATT&CK and NIST CSF. Identify TTPs your current configuration covers and which ones show misconfiguration gaps.

Time: ~20 minutes
💡 THINK FIRST · RISK FRAMEWORKS

Risk360 maps your Zscaler configuration against industry-standard threat frameworks. The MITRE ATT&CK view shows which attack techniques your controls cover — and which ones your misconfigurations leave open.

  • A Risk360 TTP tile is highlighted in blue but shows a red misconfiguration indicator. What does this combination mean?
    Q1 — MODEL ANSWER
    Blue = the technique is covered by a Zscaler protection (the product license is active and the feature is relevant). Red = the specific Zscaler configuration for that technique is misconfigured (e.g. a policy is missing, a service is unlicensed, or a recommended setting is disabled). The combination means: "Zscaler can protect you here, but your current config doesn't actually do it."
  • Risk360 shows 79% MITRE ATT&CK technique coverage. What does the remaining 21% represent, and does it mean your organization is vulnerable to those techniques?
    Q2 — MODEL ANSWER
    The 21% represents techniques that Zscaler's product portfolio does not cover — either because they are out-of-scope for a cloud security platform (e.g. physical attacks, firmware exploits) or because the customer hasn't licensed the relevant Zscaler product. It does not necessarily mean the organization is vulnerable — other security controls (EDR, network monitoring, physical security) may cover those techniques.
  • NIST CSF 1.1 and 2.0 are both supported. What is the primary structural difference between NIST CSF 1.1 and 2.0 that Risk360 would need to accommodate?
    Q3 — MODEL ANSWER
    NIST CSF 2.0 adds a sixth function, "Govern," to the original five (Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover). It also restructures subcategories and adds supply chain risk management emphasis. Risk360 must maintain separate framework versions and map its protection categories to both the old and new subcategory IDs, since organizations may be reporting against either version.

Task 12.1 — Explore MITRE ATT&CK in Risk360

1 Navigate to the MITRE ATT&CK framework view

  1. On the Corp: Client PC, log in to https://sdc.zslogin.net with the ADMIN_USER_ID and session_password.
  2. On the ZIdentity tile dashboard, click the Risk360 tile.
  3. In Risk360, click Frameworks > MITRE ATT&CK.
  4. Review the Legend:
    • Donut chart: percentage of techniques covered by Zscaler
    • Zscaler Coverage: count of covered techniques
    • Not Covered: count of uncovered techniques
    • Configured / Misconfigured: policy health
  5. Hover over any TTP tile to see the tooltip with coverage details and Risk360 factor links.
  6. Click on a TTP tile (e.g. Active Scanning) to open the drawer view.
  7. In the drawer, review: technique name, MITRE link, MITRE Description, MITRE Category, Zscaler Comment, TTP to Zscaler Product Mapping, and TTP to Risk360 Factor Mapping.

Task 12.2 — Explore NIST CSF in Risk360

2 Switch to the NIST CSF framework view

  1. In Risk360, click Frameworks > NIST CSF.
  2. Review the NIST CSF framework grid — columns: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
  3. Review the Legend: coverage percentage, configured vs misconfigured counts.
  4. Hover over any TTP tile to see the tooltip.
  5. Click on a TTP tile (e.g. Anomalies and Events) to open the drawer view.
  6. In the drawer, review the sub-techniques and their individual Status (Configured / Misconfigured / Not Covered).
  7. Note the NIST CSF link in the Details section — this links to the official NIST CSF PDF documentation for that technique.

Result: You can use Risk360 to identify which MITRE ATT&CK techniques and NIST CSF controls Zscaler covers in your environment, and which specific configurations need remediation to improve coverage.
